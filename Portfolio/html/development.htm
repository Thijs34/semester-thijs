    Recreating the awwwards website

    General info

    For the first main front-end development assignment, I Had the task to replicate one website featured on the awwwards website. The process of selecting a website to replicate had a few challenges, as I aimed to find a balance between a website that wasn't overly simplistic or excessively complex. The primary focus of the assignment was HTML and CSS, so I sought out a website that would be enjoyable to recreate and achievable primarily using CSS. I finally found a website that met my needs. It is a yacht-themed website found at this link: https://www.mangustayachts.com/

    Click the button below to see my creation before the feedback:

    awwwards website V1
    challenges

    During the project, I encountered a few challenges. The first challenge I faced was getting the correct images. Right-clicking on the website was disabled, so I had to navigate through the network-img section inside the inspector mode to retrieve the necessary files. I also encountered some issues with retrieving SVGs from the website, so I downloaded a SVG grabber tool.

    feedback

    Upon requesting feedback on the Awwwards website, I received a favorable response, with my website being praised for its aesthetics and its impressive presentation in my portfolio. It was a teacher-initiated challenge to explore the implementation of a pure CSS solution for enabling image slider functionality through drag interactions. This challenge, presented by my teacher, is something I eagerly anticipate tackling. Furthermore, I received constructive feedback on the need to make adjustments to certain semantic elements, which I will certainly take into consideration as I continue to work on this project.

    conclusion:

    In summary, this front-end development assignment, though challenging, taught me the power of creative problem-solving. While not everything matched the original site, the final product was visually appealing and fully functional. It's a valuable step in my journey as a web developer, and I look forward to applying these experiences in future projects.
