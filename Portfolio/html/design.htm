    Exploring colors

    In the first lecture of media production, we delved into the world of colors. While I had some previous understanding of fundamental concepts like primary, secondary and tertiary colors, as well as the color wheel, this lecture introduced me to a host of new terms like hue, tint, tone and shade.Additionally, I learned about various color schemes, including analogous and complementary ones, which I think will be really valuable in making decisions about colors for projects like websites and logos. Selecting the right colors is often a hard task for me, because it is a crucial decision that can impact a product's performance.

    conclusion:

    In conclusion, I learned a great deal about the importance of using the right colors. This lecture has equipped me with valuable insights for future projects, and I think I am better prepared to make informed color choices in my upcoming work.

    Exploring Image formats

    During a lecture of the media production course, I gained some valuable insights about image formats that will definitely be helpful in future projects. One significant point is this lecture was to avoid using JPEGs when editing a photo, instead use png because as it preserves the quality

    Additionally, I received a glimpse into the evolving landscape of video delivery formats. It appears that AV1 and H.264 are emerging as primary contenders in this realm. I also discovered that for video editing purposes, the DNx ProRes format is currently the way to go.

    conclusion:

    In conclusion, this lesson in media production showed me the significant importance of image formats and the evolving landscape of video delivery. You want to deliver an end product in the highest quality possible, this lecture provided me with a solid foundation to do so in future endeavors.

    Learning Typography

    During a captivating session in media production, I dove into the world of typography. Here I acquired some insights into different fonts or typefaces, distinguishing between serifs and the modern sans serif. I also learned some new terms like kerning, tracking and leading that helps with fine tuning text. To practice kerning we did another game (https://type.method.ac/) that was actually quite hard. I got 84% on my first try.

    conclusion:

    In conclusion, exploring and experimenting with typefaces has shown me its significance. Typefaces communicate messages that can greatly affect your product. Knowing this will definitely be helpful in future projects like the logo design.
