    Constructing a Survey

    To get a better understanding of the preferences and expectations of our target audience for the portfolio website we are developing for our client, we decided to construct a survey. I was in charge of creating the majority of the questions and reaching out to some art teachers and students, as they are the individuals most likely to engage with an artist or researchers portfolio website.

    When asking for some feedback on the first version of the question, I was made aware to use less open questions and use easier sentences to make sure the survey was understandable and easy to analyze. After using this feedback I was able to make some nice questions.

    Constructing personas

    conclusion:

    In conclusion, our survey efforts gave us the ability to create insightful user personas, such as the one centered around Mike, the experienced graphic designer. Our commitment to grounded research has given us a strong foundation to understand the users needs.

    Constructing my portfolio

    conclusion:

    In conclusion, conducting thorough research when developing your portfolio website is essential. It helps you explore design options, learn from successful examples, and make informed decisions about layout and content organization.
