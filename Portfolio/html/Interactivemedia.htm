Exploring Empathy mapping

In the second week of the UX journey, I had the opportunity to learn about empathy mapping. It is a nice technique that helps us gain a deeper insight into the thoughts, emotions, behaviors and needs of our target audience and users. It sort of puts yourself in their shoes to understand their perspective. This way you can make a product that really resonates with them.

I decided to do My empathy map on wanting to work out more. While I got questioned about this (what problem are you facing?, what are your thoughts?, what do you do?, how do you feel?) I gained a deeper understanding of my motivations and challenges. I was able to identify certain barriers, like the lack of knowledge of what workout to do. After showcasing the end product to the teacher I got a postive reaction, The only thing I should change in the future is asking less directed question to get even more open answers. See my empathy map by clicking on the button below...

Empathy map

conclusion:

Personally, I think empathy mapping is a great way to strengthen our professional identity. By understanding the needs and emotions of users we can create a meaningful experience. I also think it has some nice potential to be used in our main project.

Exploring Comparison analysis

In week 3 of the UX course, I delved into the concept of making comparison analysis. To practice this I decided to compare netflix and disney plus to see how they differ from each other and highlight what makes them successful. The most important comparison was the interface, because this is what the users interact with every day. The feedback on this assignment was to focus a bit more on this comparison
